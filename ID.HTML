IDIOMS


                 Idioms from A to M (this page).

                 Idioms from N to Z (next page).      

*  A

1. A Bird In The Hand Is Worth Two In The Bush:
                                               Having something
   that is certain is much better than taking a risk for more, 
   because chances are you might lose everything.

2. A Blessing In Disguise:
                          Something good that isn't recognized 
   at first.

3. A Chip On Your Shoulder:
                           Being upset for something that happ-
   -ened in the past.

4. A Dime A Dozen:
                  Anything that is common and easy to get.

5. A Doubting Thomas:
                     A skeptic who needs physical or personal 
   evidence in order to believe something.

*  B

6. Back Seat Driver:
                    People who criticize from the sidelines, 
   much like someone giving unwanted advice from the back seat 
   of a vehicle to the driver.

7. Back To Square One:
                      Having to start all over again.

8. Back To The Drawing Board:
                             When an attempt fails and it's 
   time to start all over.

9. Barking Up The Wrong Tree:
                             A mistake made in something you 
   are trying to achieve.

10. Beat A Dead Horse:
                      To force an issue that has already ended.

*   C

11. Can't Cut The Mustard :
                          Someone who isn't adequate enough to 
    compete or participate.

12. Cast Iron Stomach:
                      Someone who has no problems, complications 
    or ill effects with eating anything or drinking anything.

13. Charley Horse:
                  Stiffness in the leg / A leg cramp.

14. Chew someone out:
                     Verbally scold someone.

15. Chip on his Shoulder:
                         Angry today about something that occured 
    in the past.

*   D

16. Dark Horse:
               One who was previously unknown and is now 
    prominent.

17. Dead Ringer:
                100% identical. A duplicate.

18. Devil's Advocate:
                     Someone who takes a position for the sake of 
    argument without believing in that particular side of the 
    arguement.It can also mean one who presents a counter argument 
    for a position they do believe in, to another debater.

19. Dog Days of Summer:
                       The hottest days of the summer season.

20. Don't count your chickens before they hatch:
                                                Don't rely on it 
    until your sure of it.

*   E

21. Eighty Six:
               A certain item is no longer available. Or 
    this idiom can also mean, to throw away.

22. Elvis has left the building:
                                The show has come to an end. 
    It's all over.

23. Ethnic Cleansing:
                     Killing of a certain ethnic or religious 
    group on a massive scale.

24. Every Cloud Has A Silver Lining:
                                    Be optomistic, even difficult 
    times will lead to better days.

25. Everything But The Kitchen Sink:
                                    Almost everything and anything 
    has been included.

*   F    

26. Feeding Frenzy:
                   An aggressive attack on someone by a group.

27. Field Day:
              An enjoyable day or circumstance.

28. Finding Your Feet:
                      To become more comfortable in whatever 
    you are doing.

29. Finger lickin' good:
                        A very tasty food or meal.

30. Fixed In Your Ways:
                       Not willing or wanting to change from your 
    normal way of doing something.

*   G

31. Get Down to Brass Tacks:
                            To become serious about something.

32. Get Over It:
                To move beyond something that is bothering you.

33. Get Up On The Wrong Side Of The Bed:
                                        Someone who is having a 
    horrible day.

34. Get Your Walking Papers:
                            Get fired from a job.

35. Give Him The Slip:
                      To get away from. To escape.

*   H

36. Haste Makes Waste:
                      Quickly doing things results in a poor 
    ending.

37. Hat Trick:
              When one player scores three goals in the same 
    hockey game. This idiom can also mean three scores in any 
    other sport, such as 3 homeruns, 3 touchdowns, 3 soccer 
    goals, etc.

38. Have an Axe to Grind:
                         To have a dispute with someone.

39. He Lost His Head:
                     Angry and overcome by emotions.

40. Head Over Heels:
                    Very excited and/or joyful, especially when in 
    love.

*   I

41. Idle Hands Are The Devil's Tools:
                                     You are more likely to get in 
    trouble if you have nothing to do.

42. If It's Not One Thing, It's Another:
                                        When one thing goes wrong, 
    then another, and another...

43. In Like Flynn:
                  To be easily successful, especially when sexual 
    or romantic.

44. In The Bag:
               To have something secured.

45. In The Buff:
                Nude.

*   J

46. Jaywalk:
            Crossing the street (from the middle) without using 
    the crosswalk.

47. Joshing Me:
               Tricking me.

*   K

48. Keep An Eye On Him:
                       You should carefully watch him.

49. Keep body and soul together:
                                To earn a sufficient amount of 
    money in order to keep yourself alive .

50. Keep your chin up:
                      To remain joyful in a tough situation.

51. Kick The Bucket:
                    Die.

52. Kitty-corner:
                 Diagonally across. Sometimes called Catty-Corner
     as well.

*   L

53. Last but not least:
                       An introduction phrase to let the audience 
    know that the last person mentioned is no less important than 
    those introduced before him/her.

54. Lend Me Your Ear:
                     To politely ask for someone's full attention.

55. Let Bygones Be Bygones:
                           To forget about a disagreement or 
    arguement.

56. Let Sleeping Dogs Lie:
                          To avoid restarting a conflict.

57. Let The Cat Out Of The Bag:
                               To share a secret that wasn't 
    suppose to be shared.

*   M

58. Make No Bones About:
                        To state a fact so there are no doubts or
    objections.

59. Method To My Madness:
                         Strange or crazy actions that appear 
    meaningless but in the end are done for a good reason.

60. Mumbo Jumbo:
                Nonsense or meaningless speech.
