VOCABULARY


                     Vocabulary from A to O (this page).

                     Vocabulary from P to Z (next page).

    *  A

    1. Amnesia=Loss of memory.
    2. Atheist=A person who does not believe in the existence of god.    
    3. Autocracy=Government by one man who has unlimited powers.
    4. Arsenal=A place where weapons and ammunitions stored.
    5. Anonymous=A letter or a document which does not bear the name
                 of its writer.

    *  B
                 
    6. Bankrupt=One who is not able to payoff his debts.
    7. Bigot=A person attached loany opinion,system or party.
    8. Bureaucracy=A government in which all power is controlled by
                   the officials.
    9. Braggart=A vain,boasting fellow.
    10. Bier=Frame on which a dead body is carried or placed before
             the burial.

    *   C

    11. Cannibal=One who eats human flesh.
    12. Colleague=Person working in one and same institution.
    13. Centipede=An insect with many legs.
    14. Congregation=An assembly of worshippers.
    15. Cosmopolitan=Belonging to all parts of the world.

    *   D

    16. Deaf=A person who cannot hear.
    17. Dumb=One who cannot speak.
    18. Decorum=Dignified and socially acceptable behaviour.
    19. Diligent=Hard working.
    20. Destitution=Lack of necessaries of life.
    
    *   E

    21. Eccentric=A person with unusual or abnormal behaviour.
    22. Epigram=Short poem or saying expressing an idea in clever 
                and amusing way.
    23. Epilogue=Part added at the end of a book,play,film etc.
    24. Effeminate=A man who is having the qualities of a woman.
    25. Extempore=A speech without preparation.
    
    *   F

    26. Forgery=Signature on someone's name without his permission.
    27. Fastidious=Hard to please.
    28. Fataist=One who believes in fate.
    29. Fragile=Liable to be easily broken.
    30. Fratricide=The murderer of one's own brother or sister.
    
    *   G
 
    31. Gerontology=Science of the study of old age.
    32. Gratis=Without payment or free of cost.
    33. Garrulous=One who talks too much.
    34. Gullible=Innocent.
    35. Grandiloquence=Using a pompous style of speech.
    
    *   H

    36. Hypocrite=One who pretends to be what he is not.
    37. Hypertension=Abnormally high blood pressure.
    38. Homicide=The killing of human beings.
    39. Heterogeneous=Formed of part that are of different kinds.
    40. Honorary=Work for which no salary is paid.
    
    *   I

    41. Ignorant=One who lacks knowledge.
    42. Immobile=That which cannot be moved.
    43. Illicit=Prohibited by law.
    44. Inaudible=That which cannot be heard.
    45. Inroad=Sudden attack on another's territory.
    
    *   J

    46. Juggler=One who exhibits trick that requires skill of hands
                or eyes.
    47. Jaded=Bored or apathetic after experiencing too much of
              something.
    48. Junta=A group of officers who rule a country after seizing 
              power.
    49. Jurisdiction=The territory within which power can be 
                     exercised.
    50. Justify=Defend,explain,or make excuses for by reasoning.
    
    *   K

    51. Kindle=Cause to start burning.
    52. Kingdom=A country that is ruled by a king or queen.
    53. Keen=Very interested in something.
    54. Kitten=A young cat.
    55. Kennel=Shelter for pet dogs.
    
    *   L
 
    56. Linguist=A person who knows many languages.
    57. Legible=That which can beread easily.
    58. Loquacious=A person who talks too much.
    59. Laundry=A place where clothes are cleaned.
    60. Lexicographer=A person who compiles a dictionary.
    
    *   M

    61. Maiden=A speech which is made for the first time new.
    62. Martyr=One who undergoes the penalty of death for the shake 
               of a great cause.
    63. Mob=Crowd of unruly people.
    64. Misogynist=A woman hater.
    65. Matricide=Murderer of one's mother.
    
    *   N

    66. Notorious=Having an evil reputation.
    67. Nautical=Of ships,sailors or navigation.
    68. Nepotism=Practice of favouring ones own relatives.
    69. Nuptial=About marriage.
    70. Numismatics=The study of coins and medals.
    
    *   O

    71. Obterician=One skilled in the art of midwifery or the 
                   delivery of women in childbed.
    72. Obsolete=Out of use.
    73. Optimist=Most hopeful view of life.
    74. Oath=Solemn undertaking with God's help to do something.
    75.Orthodox=A person holding conversational beliefs specially 
                in matter of religion.
